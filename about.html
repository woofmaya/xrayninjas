Our Stories

The experiences that shape us.

Julie Kaufman

Co-captain

“My husband, Wally Niemasik, died in December 2012 of pancreatic cancer. By the time pancreatic cancer is diagnosed, it is almost always Stage 3 or 4, and most patients die within a year. Wally had a fantastic attitude and wonderful doctors, including Dr. Hofmann and Dr. Sze, and he beat the odds by living 21 months after his diagnosis. He was very helpful and inspirational to other patients during his illness. But still, his life was cut short, and earlier detection would have helped give him more time, perhaps even a normal life span. I’m riding in the Canary Challenge so patients like Wally will be able to be diagnosed much earlier. I am part of Dr. Hofmann’s and Dr Sze’s team, ‘Xray Ninjas’. It was their warrior-like attitude towards cancer that helped Wally beat the odds.” Click here to donate to Julie Kaufman.

Daniel Sze, MD, PhD

Nishita Kothary, MD

“10 more years… Imagine the gift of 10 more years after hearing the big, ugly C word. Unfortunately, today that gift is rare – a vast majority of patients live a few months past their diagnosis. Early detection can change that. 10 more years for a parent translates to giving your kids the foundation to be a good human being. 10 more years means a parent could watch his or her child graduate and go to prom. 10 more years may mean living to hold your grandchild. We may not be able to cure cancer in this lifetime, but we definitely have a shot at early detection, early treatment and loving life longer. My husband and I are riding the Canary challenge as a tribute to all the family, friends, and colleagues who have bravely faced cancer. My kids, Aria (6 years) and Riaan (3 years) are riding the canary challenge as a hope for the future. 100% of all funds raised by the Canary Challenge go directly to cancer research and innovative clinical programs. I’m very excited about riding for this cause but I can’t do it without your help. Please join me in supporting the Canary Foundation and the Stanford Cancer Institute by making a donation.” View this video to see why Dr. Kothary’s kids want to ride. Click here to donate to Dr. Kothary.

William Kuo, MD

“As a physician, I have seen first-hand the devastating effects of late-stage cancer on many patients who could have benefitted from early detection. Five years ago, I took part in the LiveStrong Challenge in honor of patients and families afflicted by cancer. Since then, I’ve been inspired to pursue charity rides every year to benefit scientific research, public health, and patient care. Last year, my father was diagnosed with cancer. Fortunately, he benefitted from early detection and received treatment that helped save his life. Sooner or later, this disease affects each of us; and important causes like the Canary Challenge help to unite us in our fight against cancer.” Click here to donate to Dr. Kuo.
